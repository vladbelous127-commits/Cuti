# Cold Outreach Sequence

Write outreach a busy stranger will actually answer. The unit of work is a sequence (3–5 touches), because first-touch reply rates are low even for great copy — follow-ups do most of the booking.

## Workflow

1. **Define the who and the why-now.** Identify: target persona (role + company type), the specific pain this persona owns, the trigger or observation that justifies reaching out now, and the ask (usually a 15–20 minute call). If the user has a prospect list or example account, personalize against it.
2. **Write the sequence.** Default structure:
   - **Email 1 (day 1)**: personalized observation → one-sentence pain hypothesis → one-sentence credibility (specific result with a similar company) → interest-based ask ("worth a look?"), under 90 words.
   - **Email 2 (day 3)**: reply in-thread, add one new piece of value (insight, stat, or a one-line case study). Under 60 words.
   - **Email 3 (day 7)**: different angle on the same pain, or a relevant resource. Under 70 words.
   - **Email 4 (day 12)**: breakup email — polite, low-pressure, leaves the door open. Under 50 words.
   - Optional LinkedIn touches: a no-pitch connection request (under 300 characters) after email 1, and a short DM after email 3.
3. **Add personalization slots.** Mark every variable as `{{first_name}}`, `{{company}}`, `{{trigger}}` etc., and list what research fills each slot (funding news, hiring page, tech stack, recent post).
4. **Include subject lines.** 2 options per email: lowercase-casual and specific. No "quick question" clichés.
5. **Attach the operating notes.** Sending guidance: 1 persona per sequence, warm up domains, personalize the first line manually, stop on any reply, and comply with local spam law (include a real opt-out path).

## Output format

Each email under a heading `Email N — day X`, with subject options, body, and a `Personalization required:` line. Finish with a sequence summary table.

## Quality bar

- The first line must prove the email couldn't have been sent to anyone else.
- No feature dumps — one pain, one proof point, one ask per email.
- Reading level: how the prospect talks, not how the product's website talks.
- Never write copy that misrepresents identity or fakes prior contact ("re:" on a thread that never existed).
