# Sales Proposal

Write proposals around the buyer's problem, not the seller's capabilities. A proposal is a decision document: its job is to make saying yes easy and defensible to the buyer's boss.

## Workflow

1. **Gather deal context.** Pull from the conversation: the client's stated problem and goal, what was promised in prior calls, decision criteria, budget signals, and deadline. Anything unknown becomes a bracketed assumption stated in the proposal, not silently invented.
2. **Write in this order:**
   1. **Executive summary** (half page max): their situation → their goal → your recommendation → the outcome and price headline. Written so a person who reads nothing else can approve it.
   2. **Understanding of the problem**: mirror their language from discovery; this section proves you listened.
   3. **Proposed solution & scope**: deliverables as bullets with acceptance criteria; explicitly list what's out of scope — scope creep starts in vague proposals.
   4. **Timeline**: phases with dates or week numbers and what the client must provide at each phase.
   5. **Pricing**: default to three options (good/better/best) with the recommended one marked — three options moves the question from "yes/no" to "which." One-option pricing only if the user insists.
   6. **Why us**: 2–3 proof points with numbers; a relevant mini case study beats a capabilities list.
   7. **Terms & next step**: payment schedule, validity date (14–30 days), and the single next action with a date.
3. **Do the buyer's-boss pass.** Reread as the economic buyer: is the ROI logic explicit? Is there a number tying price to value (payback, hours saved, revenue at stake)? Add it if missing.

## Output format

A complete Markdown document with the sections above. Use `[ASSUMPTION: ...]` markers for anything not confirmed by the user. Pricing in a table. If the user wants a Word or PDF version, format accordingly with a cover line (client name, date, validity).

## Quality bar

- The word "we" should appear far less than "you" in the first half of the document.
- Every deliverable is verifiable — "SEO audit of up to 50 pages delivered as a report" not "SEO services."
- No unexplained price: each option's price sits next to what it buys.
