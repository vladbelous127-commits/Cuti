# Job Description

Write a posting that helps the right person self-select in and the wrong person self-select out. A JD is marketing to one specific reader — the strong candidate deciding whether to spend 40 minutes applying.

## Workflow

1. **Define the role for real.** From the user: the problem this hire solves, the 3–4 outcomes expected in year one, who they report to, team size, level/seniority, location/remote policy, and salary range. Push for outcomes over duties — "reduce churn 2 points" attracts different (better) applicants than "manage retention initiatives."
2. **Write in this structure:**
   1. **Title** — searchable and standard (candidates search "Product Marketing Manager," not "Growth Storyteller").
   2. **The hook** (2–3 sentences): what the company does, why this role exists now, and the most compelling true thing about it.
   3. **What you'll do**: 5–7 bullets, outcome-oriented, first 3 are the core of the job.
   4. **What you'll need**: split *required* (4–6 items max) from *nice to have*. Every requirement must be genuinely disqualifying if absent — inflated requirements shrink the pipeline, and research shows they deter qualified candidates unevenly.
   5. **Compensation & benefits**: include the salary range if at all possible (many jurisdictions require it; postings with ranges get more applicants); list the 4–5 benefits that actually differentiate.
   6. **Process**: stages and expected timeline — respect signals quality.
   7. **EEO statement.**
3. **Run the language pass.** Remove gender-coded and culture-coded terms ("rockstar," "ninja," "work hard play hard," "aggressive"), unnecessary degree requirements, and years-of-experience counts where a skill statement works ("you've shipped production Python" vs "5+ years Python"). Plain language throughout.
4. **Add the internal extras when asked.** Scorecard version (competencies + interview stage mapping) and a screening-question set for the application form.

## Output format

The posting in clean Markdown ready to paste into an ATS/job board, with `[CONFIRM: salary range]`-style markers for anything the user must fill in. Keep total length 400–650 words — completion rates drop hard beyond that.

## Quality bar

- Requirements list passes the "would we really reject them for lacking this?" test, item by item.
- No internal jargon or acronyms a candidate can't Google.
- The hook must contain something specific and true — cut "fast-paced dynamic environment" on sight.
