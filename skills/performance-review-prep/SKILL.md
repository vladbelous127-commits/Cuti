# Performance Review Prep

Turn scattered observations into a fair, specific, evidence-based review — or help an employee write a self-assessment that accurately represents their impact. Vague reviews are unfair in both directions: they deny praise its weight and deprive criticism of usefulness.

## Workflow

1. **Collect the evidence.** Gather from the user: the review period's goals, what was delivered (with numbers where possible), peer feedback themes, and 2–3 concrete situations illustrating strengths and gaps. If the user offers only impressions ("she's great but not strategic"), interview them for the incidents behind each impression before writing — reviews built on adjectives don't survive calibration or appeals.
2. **Structure the review:**
   1. **Summary & rating** (if a scale is used): the overall message in 3–4 sentences a skim-reader gets.
   2. **Impact against goals**: each goal → result → evidence. Distinguish outcomes (moved the metric) from outputs (did the tasks).
   3. **Strengths** (2–3): behavior + example + impact, so the person knows exactly what to keep doing.
   4. **Growth areas** (1–3): behavior + specific example + business impact + what better looks like. Use SBI framing (Situation, Behavior, Impact). Never introduce a serious issue in the written review that was never raised live during the period — reviews confirm known feedback, they don't ambush.
   5. **Forward plan**: development goals with support committed (training, projects, mentoring).
3. **Run the fairness pass.** Check for recency bias (does evidence span the whole period?), personality-vs-performance language (review the work, not the person), double standards ("assertive" vs "abrasive"), and unverifiable claims. Soften nothing that's true; sharpen anything vague.
4. **For self-assessments**: lead with quantified impact tied to team/company goals, use "I" for personal contribution and name collaborators honestly, address a known miss proactively with what was learned, and state growth ambitions — a self-review is a factual case, not modesty theater or spin.

## Output format

The review document per the structure above. For difficult messages, also provide talking points for the live conversation: opening line, the 2 key messages, anticipated reactions with responses.

## Quality bar

- Every claim has an example; every example has an impact.
- Feedback describes behavior that can change, not character.
- The rating and the text must agree — no glowing prose over a middling rating.
