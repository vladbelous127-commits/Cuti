# Onboarding Plan

Build an onboarding plan that gets a new hire to independent productivity with explicit goals, named humans, and scheduled checkpoints. Good onboarding is the highest-leverage retention tool a manager has; a plan of "shadow people and read docs" wastes it.

## Workflow

1. **Anchor on the role's ramp definition.** With the user, define what "fully ramped" means for this role in observable terms (closes deals solo; ships features without review escalations; runs the monthly close). Work backward from that.
2. **Structure by horizon:**
   - **Before day 1**: equipment, accounts, access requests (these take the longest — list them first), welcome message, first-week calendar sent in advance. Nothing signals disorganization like a laptop that arrives Wednesday.
   - **Week 1 — orient**: day-by-day schedule. Mix setup, intro meetings (with a named list: manager, buddy, key collaborators, and *why* each meeting matters), product immersion, and one small real task shipped by Friday — an early concrete win beats a week of pure reading.
   - **Days 8–30 — learn**: guided work with review, systems training, sit-ins on customer calls/standups. Goal: understands how the work works here.
   - **Days 31–60 — contribute**: owns real deliverables with support. Goal: producing at ~60–70% of ramped output.
   - **Days 61–90 — own**: independent ownership of the core loop, plus one improvement suggestion (fresh eyes expire — harvest them).
3. **Make every item accountable.** Each line has an owner (new hire, manager, buddy, IT) and a due day. Assign a buddy explicitly — not the manager — for "dumb questions."
4. **Schedule the feedback loop.** Manager 1:1s at day 1, 7, 14, 30, 60, 90 with the topic for each (day 30: two-way feedback; day 60: ramp check vs. goals; day 90: performance expectations going forward). Include 2–3 questions the manager should ask at each checkpoint.
5. **Close with success criteria.** The measurable 90-day goals (3–5), agreed in week 1, that define a successful ramp.

## Output format

Checklist tables per phase (item, owner, due), the week-1 daily schedule, the 1:1 checkpoint plan, and the 90-day goals block. Mark company-specific gaps as `[FILL: system name]`.

## Quality bar

- Every meeting on the week-1 calendar states its purpose; no "intro chat" without a why.
- The plan produces a shipped artifact in week 1, however small.
- Balance load: no day with >4 hours of meetings; new hires need slack time to absorb.
