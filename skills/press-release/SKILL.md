# Press Release

Write a release a journalist can lift from — inverted pyramid, real quotes, no superlatives — plus the short pitch email that actually gets it opened. The release is the reference document; the pitch is what earns coverage.

## Workflow

1. **Find the news.** Interrogate the announcement for the actually-newsworthy angle: what changed in the world, who's affected, why now, and what number makes it concrete. "Company releases version 4.0" is not news; "4,000 clinics can now file claims in one day instead of three weeks" is. If there's genuinely no external significance, say so and suggest a better channel (blog, customer email) instead of dressing up a non-story.
2. **Write in strict inverted pyramid:**
   1. **Headline**: the news in under 12 words, no puns, no brand slogans.
   2. **Subheadline** (optional): the supporting fact or number.
   3. **Dateline + lede**: who did what, when, and why it matters — a journalist reading only this paragraph can write a brief.
   4. **Paragraph 2–3**: key details — numbers, availability, pricing if relevant, how it works in one sentence.
   5. **Quote 1** (company exec): says something with *content* — a why or a stake in the ground, not "we're thrilled." Write quotes people could plausibly say aloud.
   6. **Quote 2** (customer/partner/investor): the outside validation; concrete result preferred.
   7. **Context paragraph**: market backdrop or momentum facts (verifiable only).
   8. **Boilerplate**: 2–3 sentence company description, reused verbatim across releases.
   9. **Media contact** + assets note (press kit, images).
3. **Strip the adjectives.** Remove "leading," "revolutionary," "unique," "excited" — every claim either carries a number/fact or gets deleted. Journalists filter releases by superlative density.
4. **Write the pitch email.** Under 120 words: personalized first line referencing the journalist's beat/recent piece, the news in one sentence, why *their* readers care, and the offer (embargo, exclusive, interview access). Subject line = the news, not "Press release: …". Provide 2–3 variants for different outlet types (trade, business, local).
5. **Add the logistics block.** Recommended embargo timing, distribution notes (wire vs. direct pitch — direct beats wire for most SMBs), and a one-line social version of the announcement.

## Output format

The release in standard format (FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE / embargo line, headline, dateline, body, ###, boilerplate, contact), then pitch email variants, then logistics notes. `[CONFIRM: …]` markers on any fact not supplied by the user — never invent numbers, quotes, or customer names in a document meant for publication.

## Quality bar

- Lede answers who/what/when/why-it-matters in ≤2 sentences.
- Quotes contain information; if a quote could be deleted without losing facts, rewrite it.
- Total length 400–500 words — releases are skimmed, not read.
