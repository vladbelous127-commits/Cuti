# Interview Kit

Build a structured interview process: each competency assessed by a designated interviewer with prepared questions and an anchored scoring rubric. Structured interviews are among the best predictors of job performance; improvised chats are barely better than chance.

## Workflow

1. **Derive competencies from the role.** From the JD or user's description, pick 4–6 competencies that predict success — a mix of role skills (e.g., pipeline management), general abilities (problem solving, communication), and working style (ownership, collaboration). Each competency is assessed by exactly one primary interviewer; unassigned competencies don't get evaluated, doubly-assigned ones waste time.
2. **Write behavioral and work-sample questions.** Per competency: 2 behavioral questions (past behavior: "Tell me about a time…") each with 3 follow-up probes (situation, actions *they personally* took, measurable result), plus — for skill competencies — a work-sample element (case, exercise, portfolio walk-through) that shows the skill live. Avoid hypotheticals ("what would you do") except as follow-ups; people describe idealized selves in hypotheticals.
3. **Build anchored rubrics.** For each competency, a 1–4 scale where each level has concrete observable anchors ("4: gave a specific example with quantified outcome and articulated what they'd do differently"). Anchors turn "I liked her" into evidence.
4. **Design the loop.** Stage order (screen → skills → team → values/leadership), duration, interviewer per stage, and which competencies each stage covers — as a coverage matrix so gaps are visible. Include candidate-experience notes: what to sell in each stage, time for candidate questions.
5. **Create the debrief protocol.** Scores submitted independently *before* discussion (anchoring is real), hire/no-hire votes with reasons tied to rubric evidence, and a decision rule (e.g., any competency averaging <2 is a no).

## Output format

Coverage matrix first (stage × competency × interviewer), then per-competency sections: questions, probes, rubric table. End with the debrief form template. Note legal guardrails: no questions about protected characteristics (age, family plans, health, origin, etc.) — include a short "don't ask" list.

## Quality bar

- Every question maps to a competency; delete orphan questions however clever.
- Probes drive to "I" not "we" — the kit should say this explicitly to interviewers.
- Rubric anchors describe observable evidence, never traits ("confident") or vibes.
